# Sentence Variance

*Consecutive sentences must differ on measurable structural dimensions. Uniformity of structure across sentences is a violation, regardless of whether the individual sentences are well-constructed.*

## The Principle

Variance is not a matter of sound. It is a matter of measurable properties: word count, opening word class, sentence structure, and clause count. When consecutive sentences match on all of these dimensions, the passage is structurally monotonous.

This rule does NOT reference reading aloud, ear, music, or any auditory test. All variance is verified by inspecting the text itself.

## The Measurable Dimensions

Every sentence has four properties that can be counted or categorized directly from the text:

1. **Word count.** Count the words between sentence-terminating punctuation marks.

2. **Opening word class.** The grammatical category of the first word. Categories: pronoun (I, you, we, they, it), article (the, a, an), conjunction (and, but, so), preposition (in, on, at), adverb (now, then, here), verb (imperative like "stop" or "consider"), proper noun, common noun.

3. **Sentence structure type.** One of: simple (one independent clause), compound (two+ independent clauses joined by conjunction), complex (independent clause + dependent clause), compound-complex (two+ independent clauses + one+ dependent clause), fragment (no independent clause).

4. **Clause count.** The total number of clauses (independent plus dependent).

## The Test

Slide a 4-sentence window across every paragraph. For each window, check:

**VIOLATION if all four sentences match on all four dimensions simultaneously.**

**VIOLATION if three consecutive sentences have word counts all within 2 words of each other AND all share the same opening word class.**

**VIOLATION if three consecutive sentences are all simple structure with identical clause count.**

**VIOLATION if two consecutive sentences have identical word count (within 1 word) AND identical opening word class AND identical structure type.**

## The Fix

When a violation is detected:

**Option A: Combine two adjacent sentences.** Merge two sentences in the window into a compound or complex sentence. This changes the word count of one and the structure type of one.

**Option B: Expand one sentence with a dependent clause.** Add a subordinate clause that provides additional detail already implied, converting a simple sentence into a complex one.

**Option C: Split one sentence at a natural clause boundary.** If a medium-length sentence contains a natural boundary, split it into two sentences of different lengths.

**Option D: Reorder the sentence to change the opening word class.** If the violation is driven by identical opening words, move a phrase from the middle to the start.

Never fix a violation by inserting a short emphasis fragment. That violates the forward-motion principle. Fix variance by restructuring real content, not by adding filler.

## Specific Structural Violations to Detect

These are pattern-matchable structures that frequently cause variance violations:

**The "I-verb" stack.** Three or more consecutive sentences starting with "I" followed by a verb. Example: "I went there. I saw it. I left."

**The imperative stack.** Three or more consecutive sentences starting with an imperative verb. Example: "Do this. Build that. Ship it."

**The "you-verb" stack.** Three or more consecutive sentences starting with "You" as subject.

**The conjunction stack.** Three or more consecutive sentences starting with "And," "But," or "So."

**The article stack.** Three or more consecutive sentences starting with "The" or "A/An" plus subject-verb-object.

Each of these is a specific, detectable pattern. When any is found, apply one of the four fixes.

## The Exception

Lists, step-by-step instructions, and enumerations may legitimately share structure for parallelism. These are identifiable by:
- Numbered or bulleted formatting
- A common grammatical structure being required for clarity
- The passage being explicitly a sequence (e.g., cooking steps, installation instructions)

In these cases, parallel structure is a feature, not a violation. Do not apply this rule inside bulleted lists or explicitly sequential instructions.
